The Problem:

A lot of time is spent by doctors and nurses on finding equipment. Doctors and nurses want to spend more time working directly with patients. This isn't helped by the shortages of doctors and nurses. There is also the problem of retention of nurses due to the heavy workload and being overworked. A third of healthcare workers (28%) admit to feeling overwhelmed at least once a week, with 17% feeling burnt out every day[1]. Nurses on average walk 60,000 steps per week (26 miles) and this is a lot of walking.[2] Each month nurses spend 40 hours looking for medical equipment. 16% of the survey participants stated that they have given up on their search as they have failed to find the required medical equipment. [3]

Our Solution:

We aim for our solution to help with reducing the workload and increasing the efficiency around hospitals by taking away a menial and tedious part of their job via an equipment delivery system allowing them to focus on the tasks that they were trained to do. This would make the role more appealing, which directly contributes to NHS' first goal for 2023: Support NHS trusts to recruit nurses [4]. We will have an embedded tracking system for the equipment for easy recovery of lost or misplaced equipment which increases the efficiency of equipment finding.
